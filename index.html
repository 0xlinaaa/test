Key Finding #1: Address Verification is the #1 Bottleneck

  • ZoomEx: 100% of eligible-but-failed users (33/33) failed at Step 6 – AddressProof. Of these, 30 failed due to address verification and 3 due to WRONG_USER_REGION.
  • Matchain: 75% of failed users (6/8) also failed at AddressProof. The remaining 2 failed at ID/Passport scan.
  • This is the single most impactful step to improve — fixing address verification would recover the majority of failed users.

Key Finding #2: ZoomEx Has Significantly Lower Completion Rate

  • ZoomEx completion rate: 9.2% of eligible users vs. Matchain: 31.0% — a 3.4x difference.
  • ZoomEx has 96 users (67.6% of eligible) still pending, suggesting many users start but abandon the flow mid-way.
  • The largest pending bottleneck for ZoomEx is Step 6 – AddressProof (29 users) and Step 11 – ID Scan (19 users).

Key Finding #3: Non-eligible Users Are Almost Entirely WRONG_USER_REGION

  • Across both partners, 7 out of 8 non-eligible users were rejected for WRONG_USER_REGION.
  • Countries: Nepal (2), Thailand (1*), Morocco (1), China (1), Paraguay (1), Bangladesh (1). *Thailand data from Matchain's non-eligible with missing country.
  • Consider adding region eligibility checks before users enter the KYC flow to save effort and improve UX.

Key Finding #4: Platform Distribution

  • The vast majority of eligible users are on Web platform: Matchain 94.8% (55/58), ZoomEx 84.5% (120/142).
  • ZoomEx has 22 users (15.5%) with no platform recorded — this data quality gap is worth investigating.

Key Finding #5: Address Verification Failures Are Multi-language

  • The address verification error message appears in at least 8 languages (English, Japanese, Spanish, Russian, French, Slovak, Malay, Chinese, Burmese, Italian, Polish), indicating a geographically diverse user base.
  • This suggests the issue is systemic — not limited to one region — and may be related to the address validation service itself rather than user input quality.
